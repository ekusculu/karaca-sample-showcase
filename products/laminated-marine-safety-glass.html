Overview

Safety glass construction

Laminated glass is produced by bonding two or more glass plies with an interlayer. If breakage occurs, the interlayer helps retain glass fragments and supports safer onboard use.

Performance-led configuration

Glass thickness, PVB or SGP interlayer, coating and heated or insulated combinations can be selected according to strength, acoustic, solar and thermal requirements.

Technical range

Glass plies

  • Custom glass thicknesses
  • Typical marine panes from approx. 5–19 mm
  • Tempered or heat-strengthened options
  • Multi-layer safety configurations

Interlayers

  • PVB interlayer options
  • 0.76 mm PVB
  • 1.52 mm PVB
  • 2.28 mm PVB on request
  • SGP / DG41 high-strength option

Performance options

  • UV protection
  • Improved acoustic insulation
  • Solar control combinations
  • U-value based calculations
  • Weight-optimised SGP combinations

Applications

  • Marine windows
  • Side scuttles
  • Wheelhouse glazing
  • Double glazed units
  • Project-specific assemblies
